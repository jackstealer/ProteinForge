# Problem Identification

## The Biological Challenge
Proteins are the workhorses of biology, executing almost every function within a living cell. A protein's function is determined entirely by its 3D structure, which in turn is dictated by its 1D amino acid sequence (Anfinsen's dogma). 

However, predicting the 3D structure from the 1D sequence — often called the "Protein Folding Problem" — has been a grand challenge in biology for over 50 years.

### Limitations of Experimental Methods
Historically, protein structures could only be resolved using expensive, slow, and complex physical methods:
1. **X-Ray Crystallography:** Requires crystallizing the protein, which is notoriously difficult, especially for membrane proteins and intrinsically disordered proteins.
2. **Cryogenic Electron Microscopy (Cryo-EM):** Expensive and computationally intensive.
3. **Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR):** Limited to relatively small proteins.

Because of these limitations, the gap between the number of known protein sequences (hundreds of millions) and known protein structures (a few hundred thousand) has been growing exponentially.

## The Solution: ProteinForge
ProteinForge directly addresses this problem by democratizing access to state-of-the-art AI-driven structural biology. By integrating models like ESMFold and AlphaFold, it allows any researcher, student, or computational biologist to input a raw sequence and receive an accurate 3D structural prediction in seconds — completely bypassing the need for physical crystallography.
